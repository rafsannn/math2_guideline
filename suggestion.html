Definitions: Ordinary (ODE) vs. Partial (PDE) Differential Equations.

Properties: Identifying the Order and Degree of an equation.

Formation: Creating DEs by eliminating arbitrary constants from a family of curves.

Variable Separable (VS): Isolating variables to solve basic equations.

Reducible to VS: Using the substitution method specifically for $y' = f(ax+by+c)$.

Homogeneous DEs: Solving equations where functions $M$ and $N$ share the same degree.

Linear DEs: Utilizing the Integrating Factor (I.F.) $e^{\int P dx}$ to solve $y' + Py = Q$.

Exact DEs: Checking condition ($\frac{\partial M}{\partial y} = \frac{\partial N}{\partial x}$) and solving rules.

Constant Coefficients: Using operator $D$, Auxiliary Equations (A.E.), Complementary Functions (C.F.), and Particular Integrals (P.I.).

Homogeneous Linear: Solving Euler-Cauchy equations in format $x^2 \frac{d^2y}{dx^2} + P_1 x \frac{dy}{dx} + P_2 y = X$.

Exam Strategy & Probabilities

Maximize your score by focusing on high-yield topics. This view compares the probability of different conceptual areas appearing on the midterm. The bar chart visualizes the priority targeting, and the interactive cards below detail the exact mathematical formats you should prepare to solve.

Topic Testing Probability

🔥 High Probability (Expect These)

  • Formation of DEs: Eliminating $c$ from curves (e.g., $x^3 = c(y+c)^2$).
  • Linear DEs: E.g., $x(x-1)y' - y = x^2(x-1)^2$.
  • Exact DEs: Testing exactness and solving (e.g., $(x^2+y^2+1)dx - 2xydy = 0$).
  • Auxiliary Equations: Constant coefficient problems like $(D^3 - 3D^2 + 4D - 2)y = e^x$.

⚡ Medium Probability

  • Homogeneous Linear: ($x^n$ type) E.g., $x^2 \frac{d^2y}{dx^2} - 2x \frac{dy}{dx} - 4y = x^4$.
  • Particular Integrals (P.I.): Finding P.I. for trigonometric functions like $\sin ax$.

Expected Question Formats

Definitions & Short Qs

  • • Define ODE vs. PDE with examples.
  • • Define homogeneous linear DEs with examples.
  • • State the order and degree of a given radical or derivative equation.

Standard Numerical Formats

  • • Reducible to VS: Solve $\frac{dy}{dx} = (4x + y + 1)^2$.
  • • Reducible to VS: Solve $(2x - 2y + 5) \frac{dy}{dx} = x - y + 3$.
  • • Homogeneous: Solve $(x^2 + y^2)dx + 2xydy = 0$.

Derivations & Rules

  • • Describe the general rules to solve an exact differential equation.
  • • Explain how to solve a first-order homogeneous DE.
  • • Distinguish between C.F. and P.I.
